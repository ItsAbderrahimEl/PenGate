Gathering the Puzzle Pieces: OSINT in Penetration Testing

Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) is the practice of collecting publicly available information to build a comprehensive profile of a target. In penetration testing, OSINT helps identify potential vulnerabilities and attack vectors before direct exploitation begins.

Key Takeaways:

  • Data Sources: OSINT leverages social media, public databases, company websites, WHOIS records, leaked credentials, and more to gather valuable insights.
  • Passive Reconnaissance: OSINT is a stealthy method that does not interact directly with the target systems, minimizing detection risk.
  • Tools and Techniques: Tools like Maltego, Shodan, theHarvester, and Google Dorking automate and enhance data gathering.
  • Application: Information from OSINT guides subsequent penetration testing phases, such as phishing campaigns, network mapping, and social engineering.
