Invisible Frontiers: Wireless Attacks in Penetration Testing

Wireless networks offer convenience but also present unique security challenges. Wireless attacks target vulnerabilities in Wi-Fi protocols, misconfigurations, and weak authentication to intercept, disrupt, or infiltrate networks.

Key Takeaways:

  • Packet Sniffing: Capturing unencrypted wireless traffic to gather sensitive data or credentials.
  • Rogue Access Points: Setting up fake Wi-Fi hotspots to trick users into connecting, enabling man-in-the-middle attacks.
  • WEP/WPA Cracking: Exploiting weak encryption protocols like WEP or poorly configured WPA to gain unauthorized access.
  • Deauthentication Attacks: Forcing devices off a network to capture handshake data or disrupt communications.
