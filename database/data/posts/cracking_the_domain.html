Cracking the Domain: Active Directory Attacks in Penetration Testing

Active Directory (AD) is the backbone of many corporate networks, managing user accounts, permissions, and policies. Compromising AD can grant attackers extensive control, making it a prime target during penetration tests and real-world attacks.

Key Takeaways:

  • Kerberos Attacks: Techniques like Kerberoasting and Pass-the-Ticket exploit weaknesses in ticket-granting services to escalate privileges or move laterally.
  • LDAP and ACL Abuse: Manipulating LDAP queries or abusing Access Control Lists can expose sensitive information or allow unauthorized privilege changes.
  • Golden and Silver Tickets: Forging Kerberos tickets enables attackers to impersonate users, including domain admins, gaining persistent and stealthy access.
  • BloodHound and Enumeration: Tools like BloodHound visualize relationships and attack paths within AD, helping pentesters identify the easiest ways to escalate and move through the network.

"Dominate the domain, dominate the network." — Enterprise Red Team Wisdom

Due to AD’s complexity and critical role, attacks here can have catastrophic consequences. Pentesters simulate these attacks to identify weaknesses and help organizations secure their environments.

Defenders should implement strict privilege separation, monitor Kerberos activity, enforce strong authentication, and regularly audit AD permissions to mitigate risks.
